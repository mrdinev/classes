## üåâ Bridge ‚Äî Detail (10 minutes)

{{% notice style="bridge" title="Do Now ‚Äî expand and simplify" %}}
1. $(x + 3)(x - 2)$
2. $(2x - 1)(x + 4)$
3. $(x - 5)(x + 5)$
4. $(3x + 2)(3x - 2)$
{{% /notice %}}

**Quick recall prompt:** *"If $a \times b = 0$, what can you conclude about $a$ or $b$?"*

This leads directly into the **Null Factor Theorem** ‚Äî if $m \times n = 0$, then $m = 0$ **or** $n = 0$ (or both).

**Example:** If $(x - 3)(x + 2) = 0$, then $x = 3$ or $x = -2$.

## üîç Discover ‚Äî Detail (15 minutes)

{{% notice style="discover" title="The 3-Step Method for Solving Quadratics" %}}
| Step | What to do |
|---|---|
| **Step 1** | Write the equation in the form $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ |
| **Step 2** | Factorise the left-hand side |
| **Step 3** | Apply the null factor theorem: if $mn = 0$ then $m = 0$ or $n = 0$ |

**Remember:** always rearrange to equal **zero** first.
{{% /notice %}}

**I Do (8 min):** Solve $x^2 - x = 12$

| Step | Working |
|---|---|
| Rearrange | $x^2 - x - 12 = 0$ |
| Factorise | $(x - 4)(x + 3) = 0$ |
| Null factor | $x - 4 = 0$ or $x + 3 = 0$ |
| Solutions | $x = 4$ or $x = -3$ |
| **Check** | $x=4$: $\;4^2 - 4 = 12\;$ ‚úì $\quad x=-3$: $\;9 + 3 = 12\;$ ‚úì |

**We Do (7 min):** Students work alongside teacher ‚Äî Solve $2x^2 + 5x + 3 = 0$

*(Answer: $x = -1$ or $x = -\tfrac{3}{2}$)*
