{{% notice style="learning" title="üéØ Learning Intentions" %}}
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Identify key features of $f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c$: vertex, axis of symmetry, x-intercepts and y-intercept
- Sketch the graph of a quadratic function with all key features labelled
- Interpret the effect of changing parameters $a$, $b$, and $c$ on the parabola
{{% /notice %}}

{{% notice style="success" title="‚úÖ Success Criteria" %}}
Students demonstrate success by:
- Correctly identifying vertex and axis of symmetry $x = \dfrac{-b}{2a}$ from a given equation
- Accurately sketching at least two quadratic functions with full labelling
- Explaining in their own words how the sign of $a$ affects the parabola's shape
{{% /notice %}}

## üîç Discover ‚Äî Detail (15 minutes)

{{% notice style="discover" title="I Do (8 min) ‚Äî Example 1: Sketch f(x) = x¬≤ - 4x + 3" %}}

| Step | Working |
|---|---|
| y-intercept | $f(0) = 3 \Rightarrow (0,\, 3)$ |
| x-intercepts | $(x-1)(x-3) = 0 \Rightarrow x = 1$ or $x = 3$ |
| Axis of symmetry | $x = \dfrac{-(-4)}{2(1)} = 2$ |
| Vertex | $f(2) = 4 - 8 + 3 = -1 \Rightarrow (2,\,-1)$ |
| Concavity | $a = 1 > 0 \Rightarrow$ concave up ‚à™ |

{{% /notice %}}

**We Do (7 min):** Students work alongside teacher on $f(x) = -x^2 + 2x + 8$

**Key vocabulary:** parabola, vertex, axis of symmetry, x-intercept, y-intercept, concave up/down

**Interactive tool:** [Geogebra Parabola Explorer](https://www.geogebra.org/graphing)

## ‚úèÔ∏è Achieve ‚Äî Detail (25 minutes)

{{% notice style="achieve" title="Guided Practice (10 min) ‚Äî Q1‚Äì3 in pairs" %}}
Common errors to watch for:

| Error | What to look for |
|---|---|
| Sign error | Students use $\dfrac{b}{2a}$ instead of $\dfrac{-b}{2a}$ |
| Concavity | Students draw concave up even when $a < 0$ |
| Incomplete sketch | Missing labels on key features |
{{% /notice %}}

**Independent Practice (15 min) ‚Äî Q4‚Äì6 individually**

Targeted questioning while circulating:
- *"How do you know which way the parabola opens?"*
- *"What does the y-intercept tell you before you do any calculation?"*
- *"Can you check your x-intercepts by substituting back in?"*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Support** | Partially completed worked example scaffold |
| **Extension** | Q7‚Äì8: transformations of $f(x) = x^2$, introducing vertex form |
