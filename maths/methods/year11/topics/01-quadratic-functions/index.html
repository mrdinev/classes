Quadratic Functions - Introduction

Lesson Overview
Subject Year 11 Mathematical Methods
Topic Quadratic Functions
Duration 60 minutes
Framework VTLM 2.0 / Heathmont Model

Learning Intentions

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

  • Identify the key features of a quadratic function including vertex, axis of symmetry, and x-intercepts
  • Sketch the graph of a quadratic function in the form $f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c$
  • Interpret the effect of changing parameters $a$, $b$, and $c$ on the shape and position of the parabola

Success Criteria

Students can demonstrate success by:

  • Correctly identifying the vertex and axis of symmetry from a quadratic equation
  • Accurately sketching at least two quadratic functions with correct key features labelled
  • Explaining in their own words how the value of $a$ affects the parabola

Lesson Structure

üåâ Bridge (10 minutes)

VTLM 2.0: Enabling Learning ‚Äî activating prior knowledge

Purpose: Connect new content to students’ existing knowledge of linear functions and basic algebra.

Activity: Think-Pair-Share

Display on board:

“What do you already know about parabolas? Where have you seen them in real life?”

  • 2 minutes individual thinking
  • 2 minutes pair discussion
  • 4 minutes class share ‚Äî record responses on board
  • Teacher connects responses to today’s learning intention

Check for understanding: Ask students to sketch a rough parabola shape on their mini whiteboards. Look for common misconceptions about orientation and symmetry.

Resources needed: Mini whiteboards, markers

üîç Discover (15 minutes)

VTLM 2.0: Explicit Teaching ‚Äî I do, We do

Purpose: Direct instruction on key features of quadratic functions with worked examples.

Sequence:

  1. I Do (8 min) ‚Äî Teacher models on board:

    • Introduce $f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c$ and name each parameter
    • Work through Example 1: sketch $f(x) = x^2 - 4x + 3$
      • Find y-intercept: let $x = 0$, so $y = 3$
      • Find x-intercepts: solve $x^2 - 4x + 3 = 0$, giving $x = 1$ and $x = 3$
      • Find vertex: axis of symmetry $x = \frac{-(‚àí4)}{2(1)} = 2$, vertex at $(2, -1)$
      • Sketch with all features labelled

  2. We Do (7 min) ‚Äî Students work alongside teacher:

    • Teacher works Example 2: $f(x) = -x^2 + 2x + 8$ on board
    • Students complete same steps in their books simultaneously
    • Pause and check after each step

Key vocabulary to emphasise: parabola, vertex, axis of symmetry, x-intercept, y-intercept, concave up/down

‚úèÔ∏è Achieve (25 minutes)

VTLM 2.0: Supported Application ‚Äî guided then independent practice

Purpose: Students apply learning with decreasing levels of support.

Guided Practice (10 min):

Students work in pairs on Questions 1‚Äì3 of the worksheet. Teacher circulates, targeting common errors:

  • Sign errors when finding the axis of symmetry
  • Forgetting to check concavity (sign of $a$)
  • Incomplete labelling on sketches

Independent Practice (15 min):

Students work individually on Questions 4‚Äì6 of the worksheet.

Teacher circulates and uses targeted questioning:

  • “How do you know which way the parabola opens?”
  • “What does the y-intercept tell you before you do any calculation?”
  • “Can you check your x-intercepts are correct by substituting back in?”

Differentiation:

  • Support: Provide partially completed worked example scaffold
  • Extension: Questions 7‚Äì8 ‚Äî transformations of $f(x) = x^2$, introducing vertex form

ü™û Reflect (10 minutes)

VTLM 2.0: Enabling Learning ‚Äî consolidating and metacognition

Exit Ticket (5 min):

Students answer on a slip of paper before leaving:

  1. State the axis of symmetry of $f(x) = 2x^2 - 8x + 1$
  2. One thing I understood well today was…
  3. One thing I am still unsure about is…

Class discussion (5 min):

  • Return to learning intentions ‚Äî thumbs up/sideways/down check
  • Preview next lesson: solving quadratics using the quadratic formula

Teacher action: Review exit tickets before next lesson to identify students needing additional support.

Teacher Notes

Common misconceptions to address:

  • Students often confuse the axis of symmetry formula ‚Äî emphasise it comes from $x = \frac{-b}{2a}$, not $\frac{b}{2a}$
  • When $a < 0$, students may still draw a concave up parabola out of habit
  • Students sometimes find x-intercepts and stop, forgetting the vertex

Timing notes:

  • If the Bridge runs long, trim guided practice to 7 minutes
  • Exit tickets are essential ‚Äî do not skip even if time is short, reduce to just Question 1
