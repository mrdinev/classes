üéØ Learning Intentions

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

  • Identify key features of $f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c$: vertex, axis of symmetry, x-intercepts and y-intercept
  • Sketch the graph of a quadratic function with all key features labelled
  • Interpret the effect of changing parameters $a$, $b$, and $c$ on the parabola
‚úÖ Success Criteria

Students demonstrate success by:

  • Correctly identifying vertex and axis of symmetry $x = \dfrac{-b}{2a}$ from a given equation
  • Accurately sketching at least two quadratic functions with full labelling
  • Explaining in their own words how the sign of $a$ affects the parabola’s shape

üîç Discover ‚Äî Detail (15 minutes)

I Do (8 min) ‚Äî Example 1: Sketch f(x) = x¬≤ - 4x + 3
Step Working
y-intercept $f(0) = 3 \Rightarrow (0,, 3)$
x-intercepts $(x-1)(x-3) = 0 \Rightarrow x = 1$ or $x = 3$
Axis of symmetry $x = \dfrac{-(-4)}{2(1)} = 2$
Vertex $f(2) = 4 - 8 + 3 = -1 \Rightarrow (2,,-1)$
Concavity $a = 1 > 0 \Rightarrow$ concave up ‚à™

We Do (7 min): Students work alongside teacher on $f(x) = -x^2 + 2x + 8$

Key vocabulary: parabola, vertex, axis of symmetry, x-intercept, y-intercept, concave up/down

Interactive tool: Geogebra Parabola Explorer
